The AW3D® Global High-Resolution 3D Map, 
with the World’s Highest Accuracy
NTT DATA has jointly developed and launched AW3D® with the Remote Sensing Technology Center of Japan (RESTEC). AW3D® creates digital 3D maps displaying global topography at a resolution and accuracy of five meters. 
 
Using approximately 3 million satellite images taken by “DAICHI (ALOS),” the land-area observation technology 
satellite of the Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency (JAXA), we finished mapping topographical data for the entire 
world at the end of March 2016. This map allows consistently high-resolution analysis of terrain throughout the world, 
including mountainous regions where local surveys are problematic, which greatly increases business opportunities. In 
addition, NTT DATA has entered an agreement with DigitalGlobe, a U.S. company operating some of the world’s most 
high-performance satellites, to provide 3D data at a resolution as high as 0.5 meters in urban areas. 
 
We have provided AW3D® for use in more than 70 countries around the world, centering on emerging countries. 
The map is being used in more than 300 projects to resolve issues in a variety of fields: for mapping, disaster prevention measures, and in the area of hygiene to stop the spread of epidemics. 
 
As one example, when creating a hazard map of Macau, AW3D® elevation data enabled the accurate simulation of 
tsunami movements. The hazard map thus demonstrates what areas of reclaimed land would hypothetically be 
flooded by a tsunami. 
Responding to the Need to Set Up and 
Maintain Infrastructure in Preparation for 
Natural Disasters, Resource Development, 
and Disaster Prevention and Mitigation
In recent years, the world has experienced a number of natural disasters thought 
to be triggered by climate change, as well as earthquakes. Such disasters, concentrated on parts of Asia and Africa where infrastructure development is lagging, wreak economic and environmental damage and can impair sustainable 
development in developing countries. Around the world, some 200 million people are affected each year, with average annual economic losses due to natural 
disasters estimated at US$100 billion. This situation highlights the need to build 
societies that are resilient in the face of disaster. 
 
Major natural disasters that we face increasingly severe levels of climate 
change and threaten the health of people around the world are occurring with 
greater frequency. This situation highlights the need to put in place infrastructure 
and disaster prevention and mitigation measures, as well as effective plans to 
counter the spread of epidemics. Against this backdrop, no highly accurate topographical data covering all land is available, making it difficult in some cases to 
predict and respond to issues and enact measures to put infrastructure in place.
